What you're watching

A neutron star and black hole,
in their final moments.

~3 ms

This merger really takes about three milliseconds. What you're watching stretched out over 10 seconds is the literal final instant of something that's been spiraling in for over a billion years.

1.4 + 5 M☉

The neutron star packs 1.4 solar masses into a sphere about 24 km wide. The black hole is 5 solar masses, with an event horizon roughly the same size as the star it's about to eat. That mass range sits in what's called the "lower mass gap" — astrophysicists are still arguing about what actually lives there.

Peters '64

The inspiral you're watching runs on the Peters & Mathews quadrupole formula — the same equation LIGO uses to predict when real mergers will happen. The gravitational wave frequency rippling outward is exactly double the orbital frequency.
