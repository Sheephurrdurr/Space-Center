What you're watching

~3 ms

The real duration of this merger. What you see compressed into 10 seconds takes approximately three milliseconds in reality — the final instant of a dance that began over a billion years ago.

1.4 + 5 M☉

The neutron star carries 1.4 solar masses packed into a sphere roughly 24 km across. The black hole is 5 solar masses — its event horizon comparable in size to the neutron star itself. This puts them squarely in the "lower mass gap," a zone astrophysicists are still working to understand.

Peters '64

The orbital decay driving this inspiral is computed using the Peters & Mathews quadrupole formula — the same equation LIGO uses to predict merger timescales. The gravitational wave frequency you see rippling outward is exactly twice the orbital frequency, the dominant quadrupole mode.
